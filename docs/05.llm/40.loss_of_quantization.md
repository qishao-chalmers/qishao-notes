### 1. Main Findings
#### 1.1 Lossless Quantization
Weight-only: 4-bit is mostly lossless; 3-bit introduces >7% drop in small models.
KV cache: 4-bit maintains accuracy (<1% drop) in large models; 3-bit risky.
Weight-Activation: 8-bit safe; 4-bit can cause 2–11% drop depending on model size and algorithm (FlatQuant best).

Tables 1–5 show detailed per-model performance across all benchmarks, confirming the trends. For example, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B shows <1% drop with 8-bit WA quantization, but MXFP4 at 4-bit suffers up to 31% loss on LiveCodeBench (p. 17, Table 5).

#### 1.2 Impact of Task Difficulty
Harder tasks (AIME-120) are more sensitive to quantization than easier ones (GSM8K).

*Accuracy loss correlates with chain-of-thought length and complexity.*
Figures 6–7 (p. 18) illustrate that longer tasks generate more tokens and suffer larger degradation under low-bit quantization.

#### 1.3 Impact of Model Origin
Distillation-based and RL-based models show different robustness to quantization.
- Qwen-32B(Distillation-based reasoning model): more robust to KV cache quantization.
- QwQ-32B(Reasoning model via reinforcement learning): more robust to weight-only/WA quantization.

Pretraining, fine-tuning, and architecture differences contribute to sensitivity variations (Figures 2(a)-(f), p. 7).

#### 1.4 Output Length and Reasoning
*Quantized models generally do not produce longer outputs unless extremely low-bit.*
Low-bit quantization in smaller models can slightly increase reasoning token count (Figure 3, p. 8).

**Test-time scaling (dynamic adjustment of reasoning steps) helps improve accuracy, but gains are lower in quantized models compared to full precision (Figure 5, p. 10).**

#### 1.5 Calibration Data
Using reasoning-specific calibration data is crucial for some methods (e.g., GPTQ) due to domain shift from pre-training data like WikiText2.
KVQuant* and FlatQuant are robust to calibration domain, relying mainly on statistical properties rather than domain-specific activations (Table 2, p. 9; Figures 8–9, p. 19).

#### 1.6 Extreme Outlier Channels
Small models (1.5B, 7B) inherit large bias terms in key projections, causing extreme outliers in KV cache.
Pre-bias quantization improves performance significantly (Table 6, p. 20; Figures 10–11, p. 20–21).

#### 1.7 Scaling Effects

Model size: Larger models at low-bit precision outperform smaller BF16 models in accuracy-latency tradeoff (Figure 4a, p. 9).
Latency: Low-bit quantization speeds up inference while preserving accuracy (Figure 4b, p. 9).
Reasoning steps: Moderate increase improves performance; excessive steps can degrade accuracy due to error accumulation

## 2. [1 2025] Interpreting the Effects of Quantization on LLM

## 2.1 Accuracy and Confidence

Quantization causes minor drops in accuracy for most models (1–4%).

Exceptions:
- 4-bit Llama-2-7B: -14% on Toxicity
- 4-bit Qwen-3B: -7% on Hellaswag
- 8-bit Phi-2: -17% on IMDB

Confidence slightly drops for 4-bit models, but decoupling between confidence and accuracy is observed (Figure 2, page 6).

## 2.2 Calibration (ACE)
Quantization has minimal or inconsistent effects on calibration.
4-bit Llama-2-7B shows variable ACE across datasets, Phi-2 is more stable.
Qwen models show slight degradation under low-bit quantization, while Mistral sometimes improves.

## 2.3 Neuron Contributions
Salient neuron counts vary:
*Smaller models (Phi-2, Qwen-3B): quantization increases number of salient neurons.*
*Larger models (Qwen-7B, Mistral-7B): quantization may reduce salient neurons.*
Llama-2-7B behaves differently, likely due to hidden layer size differences (Tables 2, page 7).
Interpretation: Quantization adds noise, requiring more neurons in smaller models to maintain predictions, whereas larger models maintain richer distributed representations.

## 2.4 Dead Neurons
Quantization has minimal impact on dead neurons (Table 3, page 7).
Activation function matters: SiLU-based models (Llama, Qwen, Mistral) show almost no dead neurons, whereas GELU/ReLU-based Phi-2 has more in early layers.

## 2.5 Neuron Redundancy
Phi-2 full-precision shows highest redundancy (907k correlated pairs, Figure 4, page 8).
Llama-2-7B full-precision shows lowest redundancy, but quantized versions slightly increase it.
Differences partly due to activation functions and dead neuron counts.
